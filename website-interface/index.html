F-16V Block 70

It is a multi-purpose, single jet engine, 4th generation fighter aircraft. It was developed by General Dynamics for the US Air Force.


Today, the air forces of 29 countries in the world use different models of the F-16 fighter jet. The F-16 has undergone numerous renovations for dogfighting, the canopy has been made in the shape of a water drop for a better view, the control lever has been moved to the right to facilitate the pilot's turns with high G force, and The pilot seat is tilted backwards to reduce the G force acting on the pilot. Its armament consists of the M61 Vulcan cannon in the hull and various missiles that can be mounted on eleven loading points. It is also the first fighter aircraft capable of withstanding 9G. It can climb powerfully and accelerate vertically when necessary, provided that its thrust-to-weight ratio is not higher than 1. The F-16 is expected to remain in service with the US Air Force until 2025. F-35 Lightning II aircraft, which will be replaced by F-16s, will begin to enter service in 2011, and it is planned to gradually replace F-16s with F-35 Lightning II aircraft.
